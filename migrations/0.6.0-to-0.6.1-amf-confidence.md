# 0.6.0 to 0.6.1 - AMF record confidence

## Summary

PAM 0.6.1 makes structured confidence mandatory for every
`amf-memory/v1` record:

```json
{"score":0.99,"basis":"reviewed","assessedAt":"2026-07-12T10:00:00Z"}
```

`score` is finite and between `0` and `1`; `basis` is `observed`, `asserted`,
`inferred`, or `reviewed`; `assessedAt` is RFC 3339 UTC between the record's
creation and update timestamps.

## Existing AMF records

An existing 0.6.0 record without confidence is not silently rewritten. Before
enabling the 0.6.1 validator, migrate it through an explicitly reviewed offline
step that adds the confidence object, increments `revision`, advances
`updatedAt`, and preserves immutable claim and append-only provenance rules.
The normal proposal transition cannot perform this bootstrap after 0.6.1 is
active because a current record with missing confidence is intentionally
invalid. For a sealed record, confidence is authenticated AAD: create a fresh
AES-256-GCM envelope and IV for the migrated revision.

## Curator behavior

Curator submissions retain the numeric compatibility field, but it must equal
the validated record's `confidence.score`. The record is authoritative.
Confidence cannot grant review/apply capability and cannot expand scope or
visibility policy.
